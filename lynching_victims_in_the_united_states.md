# Lynching Victims in the United States

_This is a comprehensive list of documented lynching victims in the United States, sourced from the [Wikipedia article of the same name](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_lynching_victims_in_the_United_States). Lynching is the practice of murder by a group of people by extrajudicial action._

_The Tuskegee Institute archives hold records of 4,743 lynchings between 1882 and 1968. The Equal Justice Initiative documented 4,084 racial terror lynchings of African Americans in 12 Southern states between 1877 and 1950 alone. Many more cases went unrecorded._

---

## 2026

- **Geraldo Lunas Campos**, 55 — January 3, 2026, Texas. Latin. Illegal immigration. Tackled by multiple guards working for ICE and died while being restrained. DHS claimed Lunas Campos had attempted suicide, contrary to eyewitness reporting; the El Paso County Coroner ruled his death a homicide.

## 2020

- **Ahmaud Arbery**, 25 — February 23, 2020, Georgia. African American. Burglary. Chased down and shot. Travis McMichael, Gregory McMichael, and William Bryan convicted of murder in state court and hate crimes in federal court. The McMichaels sentenced to life without parole; Bryan sentenced to life with possibility of parole (state) and 35 years (federal).

## 2011

- **James Craig Anderson**, 47 — June 26, 2011, Mississippi. African American. Grand theft auto. Beaten, then run over with a truck. Driver convicted of murder; ten attackers pleaded guilty to federal hate crimes, receiving sentences from 7 to 50 years. Most culpable attacker Deryl Dedmon also received a life sentence for murder.

## 1990s

- **Byrd Jr., James**, 49 — June 7, 1998, Texas. African American. None (white supremacists). Dragged to death behind a car until his head hit a culvert. Three men convicted of capital murder.
- **Wilson, Christopher**, 32 — January 1, 1993, Florida. African American. None (white supremacists). Three white men kidnapped Wilson and set him on fire; he survived.
- **Rembert, Benny**, 35 — April 18, 1992, Alabama. African American. None (white supremacists). Murdered by four members of the Aryan National Front; fatally stabbed, then body dragged to train tracks.
- **Rosenbaum, Yankel**, 29 — August 19, 1991, New York. Australian Jew. Being Jewish. Stabbed to death by a mob as part of the Crown Heights riot.

## 1980s

- **Hawkins, Yusef**, 16 — August 23, 1989, New York. African American. Believed to be attending a party held by a white girl. Mob of 10 to 30 chased and beat Hawkins and friends; Hawkins was shot.
- **Kerrick Majors**, 14 — April 26, 1987, Tennessee. African American. Accidentally broke a $2 vase. Kidnapped, tortured, beaten, and stabbed to death.
- **Coggins, Wayne Timothy**, 23 — October 9, 1983, Georgia. African American. Dancing with a white woman. Stabbed 30 times and dragged to death. Case solved in 2017; two convicted in 2018.
- **Chin, Vincent Jen**, 27 — June 23, 1982, Michigan. Chinese American. Being Asian during a time when Japan was cutting into the profits of Detroit automakers. Beaten to death with a baseball bat by two white men using anti-Asian slurs.
- **Turks, Willie**, 34 — June 22, 1982, New York. African American. Drove through a majority-white neighborhood between his subway maintenance shifts. Attacked by 15 to 20 assailants who shouted racial epithets.
- **Donald, Michael**, 19 — March 21, 1981, Alabama. African American. None (Klan looked to kill a black man because accused killer of white policeman got mistrial). Three Klansmen convicted; Henry Hays was sentenced to death and executed in 1997.
- **Higdon, Benny**, 21 — May 17, 1980, Florida. White. None (killed during 1980 Miami riots). Killed by an African American mob.
- **Owens, Robert**, 15 — May 17, 1980, Florida. White. None (killed during 1980 Miami riots). Killed by an African American mob.
- **Barreca, Charles**, 15 — May 17, 1980, Florida. White. None (killed during 1980 Miami riots). Killed by an African American mob.
- **Kulp, Jeffrey**, 22 — May 17, 1980, Florida. White. None (killed during 1980 Miami riots). Killed by an African American mob.
- **Gardner, Betty**, 33 — April 12, 1978, South Carolina. African American. None (one of the perpetrators hated black people). Two white men convicted of murder, sentenced to death, and executed by lethal injection in 1998.

## 1970s

- **Pyszko, Marian**, 54 — July 28, 1975, Michigan. White (Polish). None. Killed by a group of Black youths with concrete block during riot; three alleged attackers acquitted.

## 1950s–1960s

- **Rembert, Winifred**, 19 — 1965, Georgia. African American. Fighting with deputy while in jail for stealing car to get away from two men shooting at him. Survived; became a successful leatherwork artist and died in 2021.
- **Morris, Frank**, 50 — December 14, 1964, Louisiana. African American. "Flirting" with white females.
- **Penn, Lemuel Augustus**, 48 — July 11, 1964, Georgia. African American. No accusation. Shot by members of the Ku Klux Klan.
- **Chaney, James**, 21 — June 21, 1964, Mississippi. African American. Civil rights worker. Killed by the Ku Klux Klan; contributed to passage of Civil Rights Act of 1964.
- **Goodman, Andrew**, 20 — June 21, 1964, Mississippi. White (Jewish). Civil rights worker. Killed by the Ku Klux Klan.
- **Schwerner, Michael**, 24 — June 21, 1964, Mississippi. White (Jewish). Civil rights worker. Killed by the Ku Klux Klan.
- **Parker, Mack Charles**, 22 — April 24, 1959, Mississippi. African American. Kidnapping and rape of a pregnant white woman. Taken from his prison cell and shot.
- **Till, Emmett**, 14 — August 28, 1955, Mississippi. African American. Flirting with white woman. Beaten and mutilated before being shot in the head and sunk in the Tallahatchie River. Perpetrators acquitted by all-white jury, then openly admitted they did it.
- **Banks, Isadore**, 59 — June 1954, Arkansas. African American. Being prosperous. Tied to a tree and burned alive.
- **Council, Lynn**, 19 — November 1952, North Carolina. African American. Robbery. Subjected to a mock or aborted lynching by the police; survived.

## 1940s

- **Mallard, Robert**, 38 — November 20, 1948, Georgia. African American. Voting and prosperity. Car surrounded by 20 Ku Klux Klan members; car shot at with pistols.
- **Turner, William "Wilson" Hardy**, 25 — April 20, 1948, Georgia. White. Stealing two cows from wealthy landowner John Wallace. Released from jail and beaten to death by Wallace, body cremated.
- **Gilbert, Henry "Peg"**, 42 — May 22, 1947, Georgia. African American. Hiding Gus Davidson, accused of killing a white farmer. Shot and killed by Police Chief W. H. Buchanan in Harris County Jail.
- **Earle, Willie**, 24 — February 16, 1947, South Carolina. African American. Robbery and murder of a white taxi driver. 31 suspects charged and 28 tried; all acquitted.
- **Dorsey, George W.**, 28 — July 25, 1946, Georgia. African American. Stabbing of a white man (Roger Malcom). Huge investigation; no one charged.
- **Dorsey, Mae Murray**, 23 — July 25, 1946, Georgia. African American. Stabbing of a white man (Roger Malcom). Huge investigation; no one charged.
- **Malcom, Roger**, 24 — July 25, 1946, Georgia. African American. Stabbing of a white man (Roger Malcom). Huge investigation; no one charged.
- **Malcom, Dorothy Dorsey**, 20 — July 25, 1946, Georgia. African American. Stabbing of a white man (Roger Malcom). Huge investigation; no one charged.
- **Collier, Alton**, 26 — April 27, 1946, California. African American. Alleged stabbing of a white sailor. Forced off a ferry and left to drown. Ruled a suicide until 2024 when the Equal Justice Initiative declared it a lynching.
- **Jones, John Cecil**, 31 — August 8, 1946, Louisiana. African American. Peering into a white woman's house through the window. Beaten, tortured and mutilated along with his cousin Albert Harris, Jr.
- **Drechsler, Werner**, 27 — March 12, 1944, Arizona. White (German). Collaborating with the American authorities. Hanged by seven fellow German POWs.
- **Gunther, Horst**, 23 — April 6, 1944, South Carolina. White (German). Collaborating with the American authorities. Strangled by two fellow German POWs and hanged from a tree.
- **Howard, Willie James**, 15 — January 2, 1944, Florida. African American. Sending Christmas card with "a note expressing his affection" to a white girl. Forced to jump to his death in the Suwanee River. Grand jury refused to indict.
- **Krauss, Hugo**, 24 — December 17, 1943, Texas. White (German). Collaborating with the American authorities. Beaten to death by a mob of six to 10 fellow German POWs.
- **Kunze, Johannes**, 39 — November 4, 1943, Oklahoma. White (German). Collaborating with the American authorities. Beaten to death by a mob of at least 20 fellow German POWs.
- **Tropschuh, Felix**, 29 — October 18, 1943, Kansas. White (German). Collaborating with the American authorities. Forced to hang himself by a mob of German POWs.
- **Harrison, Cellos**, 31 — June 16, 1943, Florida. African American. Murder of a white man. Awaiting new trial after conviction overturned on appeal.
- **Hall, Robert**, — January 30, 1943, Georgia. African American. Tire theft. Beaten to death by law enforcement.
- **Lang, Charlie**, 12 — October 11, 1942, Mississippi. African American. Attempted rape of a 13-year-old white girl. Taken from cells and hanged from a bridge after pleading guilty at preliminary hearing.
- **Green, Ernest**, 12 — October 11, 1942, Mississippi. African American. Attempted rape of a 13-year-old white girl. Taken from cells and hanged from a bridge after pleading guilty at preliminary hearing.
- **Wright, Cleo**, 26 — January 25, 1942, Missouri. African American. Attempted rape of a white woman and attempted murder of a white police officer. Burned alive in front of two black church congregations.
- **Hall, Felix**, 19 — February 1941, Georgia. African American. Unknown. Hanged from a tree in a ravine.
- **Callaway, Austin**, — September 8, 1940, Georgia. African American. Attempted rape of a white woman. Shot.
- **Thornton, Jesse**, 26 — June 22, 1940, Alabama. African American. Failure to address a white police officer as "Mr." Shot and thrown into the Patsaliga River.
- **Williams, Elbert**, 31 — June 20, 1940, Tennessee. African American. Registering to vote and starting an NAACP chapter. Last reported lynching in Tennessee.

## 1930s

- **Brown, Miles Wilson**, 25 — April 1, 1939, Florida. African American. Murder of his employer. Taken from jail by a mob and shot after conviction with recommendation of mercy.
- **Gooden, Albert**, 35 — August 16, 1937, Tennessee. African American. Murder of a white police officer. Taken from sheriff by 100 men and lynched from bridge over Beaver Creek.
- **Ponder, Ernest**, 14–18 — July 19, 1937, Florida. African American. Broke into a store, accused of attacking a police officer with a knife. Four masked men kidnapped them from jail, shot them dozens of times.
- **Hawkins, Richard**, 16 — July 19, 1937, Florida. African American. Same case as Ponder above.
- **Townes, Roosevelt**, 26 — April 13, 1937, Mississippi. African American. Robbery and murder of a white store owner. Tied to a tree and tortured with blowtorches; burned alive.
- **McDaniels, Robert**, 20 — April 13, 1937, Mississippi. African American. Same case as Townes above.
- **Bell, Baxter**, 45 — November 4, 1935, Tennessee. African American. Slapping a white woman. Overpowered the sheriff escorting him and shot.
- **Higginbotham, Elwood**, 28 — September 17, 1935, Mississippi. African American. Killed a white man in self-defense after he attacked him for complaining about cattle running over his field.
- **Johnson, Clyde L.**, 24 — August 3, 1935, California. White. Killing of Police Chief Frank R. Daw. Hanged from a tree near Yreka.
- **Stacey, Reuben**, 37 — July 19, 1935, Florida. African American. Threatening and frightening a white woman with a pen knife. Law enforcement officer involved; grand jury refused to indict.
- **Morton, Dooley**, 17 — July 13, 1935, Mississippi. African American. Attempted rape of a white woman. Hanged from a tree.
- **Moore, Bert**, 25 — July 13, 1935, Mississippi. African American. Attempted rape of a white woman. Hanged from a tree.
- **Neal, Claude**, 23 — October 26, 1934, Florida. African American. Rape and murder of a white woman. Castrated, forced to consume his genitals, stabbed, burned with hot irons, toes and fingers removed, hanged, body tied behind automobile.
- **Johnson, Robert**, 40 — January 30, 1934, Florida. African American. Assault on white woman. Investigators determined charges were meritless, then released him to a lynch mob.
- **Cheek, James Cordie**, 17 — December 15, 1933, Tennessee. African American. Attempted rape. Mutilated and hanged after being cleared by a grand jury.
- **Gregory, David**, 25 — December 7, 1933, Texas. African American. Rape and murder of a white woman. Shot, dragged behind a car, burned at the stake. A white man later allegedly confessed on his deathbed.
- **Thurmond, Thomas Harold**, 27 — November 26–27, 1933, California. White. Kidnapping and murder of department store heir Brooke Hart. An estimated 10,000 people witnessed the lynching.
- **Holmes, John M.**, 29 — November 26–27, 1933, California. White. Same case as Thurmond above.
- **Armwood, George**, 23 — October 18, 1933, Maryland. African American. Attempted assault and rape. Grand jury declined to indict identified lynchers. Last lynching in Maryland.
- **Lawrence, Elizabeth**, — June/July 1933, Alabama. African American. Reprimanding a group of white children who threw stones at her.
- **Dendy, Norris**, 33 — June 4–5, 1933, South Carolina. African American. Striking a white man following an argument. Broken out of jail; five white men indicted but none convicted.
- **Micou, Reuben**, 65 — April 2, 1933, Mississippi. African American. Getting into an altercation with a white man. Abducted from jail; injuries suggested he was whipped before being shot multiple times.
- **Read, Pleasant Richard**, 53 — April 17, 1932, Kansas. White. Kidnapping, rape, and murder of an 8-year-old white girl. A mob of 200 farmers broke into the jail and hanged him from a tree. Last lynching in Kansas.
- **Kahahawai, Joseph**, 22 — January 7, 1932, Hawaii. Hawaiian. Rape of a white woman. Abducted, beaten, and shot; later posthumously cleared.
- **Thompson, Shedrick**, 39 — 1932, Virginia. African American. Rape of a white woman and assault of her husband. Body found hanged and burnt; local authorities claimed suicide.
- **Tillis, Dave**, 52 — 1932, Texas. African American. "Demanded an accounting from his landlord. Charged with 'entering the bedroom of a white woman.'"
- **Mendiola, Higinio**, 46 — December 29, 1931, Texas. Latino. None. Hanged from a tree near his home to appear as suicide to collect insurance.
- **Williams, Matthew**, 23 — December 4, 1931, Maryland. African American. Killing his employer. Taken forcibly from hospital; no indictment despite numerous witnesses.
- **Bannon, Charles**, 22 — January 29, 1931, North Dakota. White. Murdering his employer and family. Mob broke into jail and hanged him from a bridge.
- **Gunn, Raymond**, 27 — January 12, 1931, Missouri. African American. Murdering a white woman. Burned to death; National Guard stood by and watched.
- **Parker, John**, — 1931, Arkansas. African American. Stealing some peaches.
- **Wise, Mrs.**, — 1931, Virginia. African American. Objected to her daughter being taken out for "rides" with white Klansmen.
