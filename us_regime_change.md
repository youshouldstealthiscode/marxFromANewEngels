# United States Involvement in Regime Change

_This is a comprehensive list of documented US involvement in regime change operations worldwide, sourced from the [Wikipedia article of the same name](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_involvement_in_regime_change). The United States has been involved in overt and covert regime change operations across every inhabited continent._

---

## 2020s

- **Iran** — 2026. US and Israel launched major strikes on Iran with the stated goal of regime change. On February 28, 2026, Iranian Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei was killed in an Israeli missile strike.
- **Cuba** — 2026. US caused oil shortage in Cuba during the Venezuela intervention, aiming to remove the Cuban government by the end of the year.
- **Venezuela** — 2025–2026. Operation Southern Spear. Trump authorized CIA covert operations in Venezuela in October 2025. On January 3, 2026, the US conducted airstrikes across Venezuela including Caracas, and captured and exfiltrated President Nicolás Maduro and his wife Cilia Flores.
- **Greenland** — 2025–2026. Trump renewed annexation threats against Greenland, refusing to rule out military force. The US set up intelligence agencies and influencer networks to conduct covert influence operations in Greenland, resulting in two US ambassador summons by Denmark.
- **Bolivia** — 2019. Alleged support for ouster of President Evo Morales. Morales claimed the US plotted to oust him for pursuing lithium extraction partnerships with Russia and China. DOJ pressured MIT researchers whose work argued Morales' election was legitimate.
- **Syria** — 2011–2017. Timber Sycamore. CIA program provided training, weapons, and billions in funding to vetted moderate Syrian rebels to overthrow Assad. Program "phased out" by Trump in July 2017.
- **Libya** — 2011. Operation Odyssey Dawn. France, US, and UK launched airstrikes and over 110 Tomahawk cruise missiles. NATO took over (Operation Unified Protector). Gaddafi was captured and killed in October 2011, ending his 42-year rule.
- **Somalia** — 2003–2007. CIA covert operations to overthrow the Islamic Courts Union (ICU). The US funded Somali warlords via the Alliance for the Restoration of Peace and Counter-Terrorism. After that failed, the US backed an Ethiopian invasion in December 2006, deploying US Special Forces and AC-130 gunships. The ICU was overthrown.

## 2000s

- **Honduras** — 2009. US military officials from the Center for Hemispheric Defense Studies quietly assisted Honduran officers in the June 28, 2009 coup d'état against President Manuel Zelaya.
- **Syria** — 2005–2009. State Department funneled at least $6 million to opposition groups including Barada TV and the Movement for Justice and Development in Syria, as revealed by WikiLeaks.
- **Palestine** — 2006–2007. After Hamas won the 2006 Palestinian legislative elections, the Bush administration pressured Fatah to topple the Hamas government of PM Ismail Haniyeh. The US provided secret training, armaments, and tens of millions in congressional funding to Fatah.
- **Haiti** — 2004. Support for coup against Aristide. Rebels were allegedly trained by US Special Forces in the Dominican Republic. Aristide alleged the coup was orchestrated by France and the US. A US-led Multinational Interim Force invaded Haiti.
- **Kyrgyzstan** — 2005. Tulip Revolution. The US government provided aid to opposition protesters via State Department, USAID, Radio Liberty, and Freedom House. Mass protests ousted President Askar Akayev.
- **FR Yugoslavia (Serbia)** — 2000. Bulldozer Revolution. US State Department actively supported opposition groups such as Otpor through funding via quasi-NGOs. Clinton authorized CIA involvement; the CIA funneled "certainly millions of dollars" into the anti-Milošević campaign. Milošević was defeated and overthrown.
- **Haiti** — 1994–1995. Operation Uphold Democracy. US obtained UN Security Council Resolution 940 authorizing an invasion. Diplomatic ultimatum by Carter and Powell led to dictator Cédras's capitulation. US military stabilized the country and restored Aristide.
- **Haiti** — 1991. CIA paid key members of the coup regime forces. Coup leaders Raoul Cédras and Michel François had received US military training. Aristide was deposed eight months after his election.

## 1990s

- **Zaire (DRC)** — 1996–1997. US covertly supported Rwanda and the Alliance of Democratic Forces for the Liberation of Congo. The US sent soldiers to train the Rwandan Patriotic Front, brought FPR commanders to the US for training, and set up communications in Uganda. Mobutu was overthrown in May 1997.
- **Iraq** — 1992–1996. Operation DBACHILLES. CIA launched coup operation recruiting Ayad Allawi and the Iraqi National Accord. CIA directed sabotage and bombing campaign in Baghdad (1992–1995). Allawi's 1996 coup attempt failed.
- **Iraq** — 1991. After the Gulf War, the US broadcast signals via the CIA-funded "Voice of Free Iraq" encouraging Iraqis to rise up against Saddam Hussein. Shia and Kurdish uprisings erupted but the US did not provide direct support; the Shia uprisings were crushed.
- **Russia** — 1996. US electoral intervention supporting Boris Yeltsin's re-election, with the US funneling aid and campaign support through the NED and other channels.

## 1980s

- **Panama** — 1989–1994. Operation Just Cause. US invaded Panama after Noriega nullified the 1989 election results. Noriega was deposed, and the elected government of Guillermo Endara was installed.
- **Haiti** — 1991. (See above.)
- **Grenada** — 1983. Operation Urgent Fury. US and a coalition of six Caribbean nations invaded Grenada and overthrew the Marxist government. UN General Assembly called it "a flagrant violation of international law."
- **Chad** — 1981–1982. Reagan gave covert CIA support to Hissène Habré when he returned from exile to fight head of state Goukouni Oueddei. Habré overthrew Oueddei on June 7, 1982. CIA continued supporting Habré, including training his notorious secret police (DDS).
- **Nicaragua** — 1981–1990. US armed, trained, and funded the Contras to overthrow the Sandinista government. CIA distributed the "Psychological Operations in Guerrilla War" manual instructing Contras on assassinating judges and blowing up buildings. US also mined harbors. The ICJ ruled against the US in 1986.
- **Angola** — 1975–1991. Operation IA Feature. US covertly supported UNITA (led by Jonas Savimbi) and the FNLA against the leftist MPLA. Ford approved $39 million in covert funding in 1975. Reagan approved $25 million including Stinger missiles. US involvement ended with the 1991 Bicesse Accords.
- **Afghanistan** — 1979–1992. Operation Cyclone. CIA armed and funded Afghan mujahideen to overthrow the Soviet-backed government. CIA-funded beneficiaries included Islamist commanders Jalaluddin Haqqani and Gulbuddin Hekmatyar, key allies of Osama bin Laden. Combined US, Saudi, and Chinese aid totaled an estimated $6–12 billion.
- **Cambodia** — 1970–1979. US supported Lon Nol's coup against Prince Sihanouk. Nixon and Kissinger approved intensified US bombing (Operations Menu and Freedom Deal), causing mass civilian casualties used by the Khmer Rouge for recruitment. The weakened Khmer Republic collapsed to the Khmer Rouge in April 1975, leading to the Cambodian genocide.
- **East Timor** — 1975–1999. Ford and Kissinger gave Suharto the green light to invade East Timor one day before the December 7, 1975 invasion. American weapons were crucial to the invasion. US military aid continued throughout the 24-year occupation. The Commission for Reception, Truth and Reconciliation concluded US "political and military support were fundamental to the invasion and occupation."
- **Chile** — 1970–1973. Project FUBELT. CIA ran psy ops from 1963 through the 1973 coup, with CIA involvement in every Chilean election during that time. Nixon promised to "make the economy scream." CIA-backed kidnapping attempt of General Schneider preceded Allende's inauguration. On September 11, 1973, President Allende was overthrown by General Pinochet with US support.
- **Argentina** — 1976. The Argentine Armed Forces overthrew President Isabel Perón. The coup and regime were endorsed by the US, with Kissinger paying official visits during the dictatorship. Part of Operation Condor; associated with the Dirty War.
- **Bolivia** — 1971. US government supported the coup led by General Hugo Banzer that toppled President Juan José Torres. Torres was later kidnapped and assassinated in 1976 as part of Operation Condor.
- **Australia** — 1975. Alleged CIA involvement in the Whitlam dismissal. Governor-General John Kerr dismissed Prime Minister Gough Whitlam, who had threatened to close US military bases. Former CIA officers have described Kerr as "our man Kerr."

## 1970s

- **Indonesia** — 1965–1967. US supported the mass killings and overthrow of Sukarno. US Ambassador Green encouraged military leaders to act forcefully. An estimated half million to over a million people were killed. In 1990, US diplomats admitted providing the Indonesian army with thousands of names of alleged Communist supporters. Sukarno was forced out in 1967 and replaced by Suharto.
- **British Guiana (Guyana)** — 1964. CIA conducted a covert political campaign against left-wing PM Cheddi Jagan. CIA supported trade unions during a 1963 strike, funded rival political parties, and established the Justice Party as a CIA front. Political violence escalated with nearly 200 murders.

## 1960s

- **Dominican Republic** — 1965–1966. Operation Power Pack. US military occupation during the Dominican Civil War, intervening to prevent what the US characterized as a potential Communist takeover after a constitutionalist revolt.
- **Brazil** — 1964. Operation Brother Sam. US supported the military coup overthrowing President João Goulart. The US sent its Navy and Air Force to help military rebels. A right-wing military dictatorship ruled until 1985.
- **Congo-Leopoldville (DRC)** — 1960–1965. CIA sent poison to station chief Larry Devlin for use against Prime Minister Patrice Lumumba. Lumumba was subsequently killed. The US then funded Mobutu Sese Seko. Mobutu launched a second coup in 1965 with US support, becoming dictator for 32 years.
- **Dominican Republic** — 1961. US-supplied weapons were used to kill dictator Rafael Trujillo. CIA described its role as a "success" in moving the country from a totalitarian dictatorship.
- **Cuba** — 1959–1962. Bay of Pigs Invasion (1961) and Operation Mongoose. CIA-backed force of Cuban exiles invaded Cuba but was defeated within three days. Operation Mongoose included embargo, sabotage of civilian targets, and multiple assassination attempts on Fidel Castro.
- **South Vietnam** — 1959–1963. US sanctioned and encouraged the generals' coup against Diem. Cable 243 on August 24, 1963 gave the green light. The November 1–2, 1963 coup resulted in Diem's assassination. The Pentagon Papers confirmed: "we variously authorized, sanctioned and encouraged the coup efforts."
- **Laos** — 1960. After Captain Kong Le seized Vientiane in a neutralist coup, the CIA supported a counter-coup through Thailand. CIA supplied artillery, artillerymen, and advisers to General Phoumi Nosavan, and deployed Air America.
- **Iraq** — 1963. Ba'athist Ramadan Revolution. Widely believed the CIA provided the Ba'athist National Guard with lists of Communists, who were then arrested or killed during the anti-Communist purge. Ba'athist leaders maintained supportive relationships with US officials.
- **Iraq** — 1959. US supported Egyptian efforts to topple Qasim. An unsuccessful October 7, 1959 assassination attempt involving a young Saddam Hussein was reported as a CIA-Egyptian intelligence collaboration.
- **Guatemala** — 1954. Operation PBSuccess. CIA coup overthrowing elected President Jacobo Árbenz and installing Carlos Castillo Armas, the first of a line of right-wing dictators. Motivated by fear of reduced profits to the United Fruit Company.
- **Guatemala** — 1952. Operation PBFortune. Aborted CIA covert operation to overthrow President Árbenz, authorized by Truman.
- **Indonesia** — 1957–1959. CIA supported the Permesta Rebellion against Sukarno. CIA pilots bombed civilian and military targets. CIA pilot Allen Pope was shot down and captured, revealing US involvement.
- **Syria** — 1956–1957. Three failed CIA coup attempts: Operation Straggle (1956), Operation Wappen (1957, planned by Kermit Roosevelt Jr. to assassinate Syrian officials), and "The Preferred Plan" (never carried out).
- **Cambodia** — 1959. Bangkok Plot. CIA supported covert attempt to overthrow Sihanouk with South Vietnam and Thailand. Interior Minister Dap Chhuon was captured and executed.
- **Egypt** — 1952. CIA coordinated with the Free Officers' coup. Kermit Roosevelt Jr. was dispatched to meet King Farouk. CIA officer Miles Copeland recounted that Roosevelt helped coordinate the coup that replaced the monarchy with a republic.
- **Iran** — 1952–1953. Operation Ajax. CIA and MI6 orchestrated the overthrow of democratically elected PM Mohammad Mosaddegh, who had nationalized Iranian oil. The CIA admitted its role "as an act of US foreign policy" in 2013.
- **Syria** — 1949. CIA-backed March 1949 coup. Government of Shukri al-Quwatli was overthrown by Husni al-Za'im, who had extensive CIA connections. Za'im approved the TAPLINE oil pipeline.
- **Costa Rica** — 1948. US supported the opposition in the Costa Rican Civil War against reformist President Calderón Guardia.

## 1950s

- **Philippines** — 1953. US electoral intervention supporting Ramon Magsaysay's election.
- **Japan** — 1950s–1960s. CIA spent millions supporting Japanese right-wing parties in national elections.
- **Lebanon** — 1957. US electoral intervention.
- **Burma and China** — 1950–1953. Operation Paper. CIA assisted Kuomintang (Nationalist Chinese) forces retreating to northern Burma, planning incursions into Western China under General Li Mi.

## 1940s

- **Philippines** — 1944–1945. US landings ended the Japanese occupation and overthrew the puppet Second Philippine Republic.
- **Japan** — 1941–1952. Overthrow of the Empire of Japan / occupation. MacArthur's command dictated a new constitution renouncing aggressive war.
- **Germany** — 1941–1949. Overthrow of Nazi Germany / Allied occupation and denazification.
- **Italy** — 1941–1946. Allied invasion / overthrow of Mussolini's fascist regime.
- **Italy** — 1948. US electoral intervention to prevent Communist electoral victory.
- **South Korea** — 1945–1948. US military government established; outlawed the People's Republic of Korea committees; backed Syngman Rhee's authoritarian election.

## Pre-1940s

- **Russia** — 1918–1920. Allied intervention in the Russian Civil War. Wilson sent 5,000 troops to North Russia (Polar Bear Expedition) and 8,000 to Siberia to support the White movement and overthrow the Bolsheviks.
- **Nicaragua** — 1912–1933. US military occupation and control. Fought Augusto César Sandino's rebels. Left the Somoza family in power, who killed Sandino in 1934.
- **Dominican Republic** — 1916–1924. US military occupation. US Marines invaded and controlled the Dominican military and police.
- **Haiti** — 1915–1934. US occupation at the request of US-based banks. The US installed a new government and dictated a new Haitian constitution. Fought the Caco Wars against resistance.
- **Cuba** — 1906–1909. Second US occupation after President Estrada Palma's government collapsed amid electoral fraud allegations. Secretary of State Taft invaded the country under the Platt Amendment, governing under Charles Edward Magoon until overseeing the election of José Miguel Gómez in 1909.
- **Mexico** — 1913. US Ambassador Henry Lane Wilson actively supported the "Ten Tragic Days" coup overthrowing democratically elected President Francisco I. Madero.
- **Nicaragua** — 1909–1910. US supported Conservative revolt against President Zelaya, who had sought to work with Germany or Japan on a competing canal. Zelaya forced to resign.
- **Honduras** — 1903–1925. Multiple US military incursions, including supporting coups by Manuel Bonilla (1903, 1911–1912). Driven by United Fruit Company and Standard Fruit Company interests.
- **Philippines** — 1899–1902. Philippine–American War. US dissolved the self-governing Philippine Republic and established an Insular Government.
- **Cuba** — 1898–1902. First US occupation after the Spanish-American War. US military occupied Cuba and imposed the Platt Amendment, giving the US the right to intervene in Cuban affairs.
- **Panama (Colombia)** — 1903. US supported Panamanian independence/secession from Colombia. Roosevelt dispatched USS Nashville to facilitate the secession, securing the future Canal Zone.
- **Kingdom of Hawaii** — 1893. Anti-monarchists, mostly Americans, overthrew Queen Lili'uokalani. Hawaii was reconstituted as a republic with the goal of US annexation, accomplished in 1898.
- **Mexico** — 1865–1867. US sent arms and supported Americans fighting alongside Benito Juárez's Liberal forces against French-installed Emperor Maximilian I.
- **Mexico** — 1846–1848. Mexican–American War. US annexed the Republic of Texas and seized Alta California from Mexico.
